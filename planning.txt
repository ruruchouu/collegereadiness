<h2>Planning</h2>
<p>The next 4 years of high school or however many years you have possess a distinct guiding force on how you pick your remaining classes and eventually the type of major or career course most suited for your future. Therefore, planning out your activities ahead of time is a very essential part to your high school career. </p> 

<!-- Listing them out -->

  
<h2>A-G Requirements </h2>
<p>CA high schools and online schools are required to satisfy the "a-g" subjects requirements approved by the UC</p>

<ul>
<li><strong>History/social science</strong> (a) &#45; Two years, including one year of world history, cultures and historical geography and one year of U.S. history, or one-half year of U.S. history and one-half year of  American government or civics.
</li>
<li><strong> English </strong> (b) &#45; Four years of college preparatory English that integrates reading of classic and modern literature, frequent and regular writing, and practice listening and speaking.</li>
<li><strong> Mathematics</strong> (c) &#45;Three years of college&#45;preparatory math, including or integrating the topics covered in elementary and advanced algebra and two&#45; and three&#45;dimensional geometry.
</li>
<li><strong> Laboratory science</strong> (d) &#45; Two years of laboratory science providing fundamental knowledge in at least two of the three disciplines of biology, chemistry and physics.
</li>
<li><strong> Language other than English </strong> (e) &#45; Two years of the same language other than English or equivalent to the second level of high school instruction.
</li>
<li><strong> Visual and performing arts </strong> (f) &#45; One year chosen from dance, music, theater or the visual arts.
</li>
<li><strong> College-preparatory elective </strong> (g) &#45; One year chosen from the "a&#45;f" courses beyond those used to satisfy the requirements above, or courses that have been approved solely in the elective area.</li>
</ul>

<h2>Freshman Year</h2>
<ol> 
 <li><em> OPTION</em>: Do geometry or spanish 1 or ceramic early. Depends on what is offered. </li>
 <li>Start by familiarizing yourself with the school grounds. Visit the library, the office, the different subject buildings, and the lunch area. Check out the special showcases and Orientation Day.</li>
 <li>Check the main website. Navigate to look up different teachers, the counselors, and other relevant information.</li>
 <li>Try to figure out which clubs you would like to join based on the handouts you receive on the book pickup and schedule registration day.</li>
 <li>Visit these clubs and maintain a regular schedule for it. CSF is highly recommended.Even make a club of your own!!</li>
<li> Maintain good study habits.</li>
<li>Get involved in a sport or other extracurricular activity.</li>
<li>Start community service hours. Make copies and turn in.</li>
<li><em>OPTION</em>: Take spanish 2 over the summer to get a head start for SL Spanish and your IB Diploma. </li>
<li> Do well in PE so you will not have to repeat the experience.</li>
</ol>

<h2>Sophomore Year</h2>
<ol> 
 <li>Maintain your grades and community service hours.</li>
 <li>Check your teacher's websites for updates on the calendar.</li>
 <li>Check the grade portal to make sure none of your assignments are missing</li>
 <li>Keep visiting the clubs!</li>
 <li> Amp up classes: around 2 &#45; 3 AP classes,  maybe some pre&#45;AP,or even SL spanish early.</li>
 <li>Stay committed to sport or extracurricular. Try to aim for leadership positions.</li>
<li>Finish up PE.</li>
<li><em>RECOMMENDED</em>: Take a PSAT or practice ACT during this year.Take at least one SAT subject test.</li>
</ol>

<h2>Junior Year</h2>
<ol> 
 <li>Maintain your grades and community service hours.</li>
 <li>Check your teacher's websites for updates on the calendar.</li>
 <li>Check the grade portal to make sure none of your assignments are missing.</li>
 <li>Keep visiting the clubs and gain leadership positions!</li>
 <li> Amp up classes: around 3 &#45; 4 AP classes; IB Certificate and AP class mix; IB full Diploma.</li>
 <li>Stay committed to sport or extracurricular. Try to aim for leadership positions.</li>
<li>Fulfill your VAPA this year.</li>
<li><em>RECOMMENDED</em>: Take at least one SAT or ACT during this year.</li>
<li><em>RECOMMENDED</em>: Take at least one SAT subject test.</li>
<li><em>RECOMMENDED</em>: Research more about the colleges you hope to apply to and the majors they have. </li>
</ol>

<h2>Senior Year</h2>
<ol> 
<li><strong>HIGHLY RECOMMENDED</strong> Do your college app essays and letter of recommendation packet over the summer.</li>
<li>Schedule your senior pictures with the studio during the summer!! DO NOT wait. </li>
 <li>Maintain your grades and community service hours. Make sure all 40 hours are turned in.</li>
<li>Look at your senior activities!!</li>
 <li>Check your teacher's websites for updates on the calendar.</li>
 <li>Check the grade portal to make sure none of your assignments are missing.</li>
 <li>Keep visiting the clubs and maintain leadership positions!</li>
 <li> Amp up classes: around 4 &#45; 5 AP classes; IB Certificate and AP class mix; IB full Diploma.</li>
 <li>Stay committed to sport or extracurricular. Keep for leadership positions.</li>
<li>Fulfill your VAPA this year.</li>
<li><em>RECOMMENDED</em>: Check if you need to take the SAT or ACT one last time before the application deadlines in November or December for college.</li>
<li><em>RECOMMENDED</em>: Check out the college campuses.</li>
<li>Doublecheck your college admissions.</li>
<li>Complete the senior exit project.</li>
</ol>
