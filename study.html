Study Habits

Studying is absolutely essential skill to have in high school but not all students do it correctly. It is very important to take a look at what is being done right and what is being done wrong.

Tips to Improve

  • Calendar is a necessity. Staying organized depends highly on keeping it updated properly and following all homework and projects set for the day and throughout the weeks.
  • Ensure that a quiet study space is made at home or even go to the library to find the right environment.
  • Keep social media and distractions such as phones and other objects far from reach. When taking breaks, go out for a breath of fresh air or exercise in place, but make sure to keep a time limit to remind you to return.
  • Do not be afraid to ask for help. After school tutoring in the library is a resource as well as your teachers themselves.
  • Look up reliable additional resources from the internet.

Resources

Thomas Frank or College Info Geek Tips: Optimize your Learning Habits
Cal Newport Study Tips
Scott H Young: The Man who did the MIT Challenge

I highly recommend the above websites. Remember to not only use one style of learning or studying. Try other styles to see which aspect is easiest for the subject.

A test for understanding could be to see if you could possibly simplify the subject or theme to terms simple enough for a young child to understand. Either verbally or textually, try to express those thoughts based on recall alone. Rank yourself from 1- 10, 10 being excellent and easy to understand to 1 being barely comprehensible or lacking in sufficient information.

  • Try listening to an audiobook or recording of your own voice for English or History. Test if it helps you.
  • Make study guidesfor each unit or framework objective. I recommend the QEC Method . This method utilizes Question, Evidence, and Conclusion to help smoothen the study process.
  • Use the method of loci. You visalize an image and attach it to each concept you hope to memorize.Then visualize the images in order to form a cohesive map and repeat the visualization process for recall and memorization. Best done with simple concepts and ideas.
